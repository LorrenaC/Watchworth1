The Drama

The Drama is a dark romantic comedy that starts off like a typical charming “pre-wedding couple” story before gradually turning into something much more uncomfortable and psychologically tense. The film stands out for its strong performances—especially Zendaya and Robert Pattinson—who carry the shifting tone between humor, awkwardness, and emotional distress very effectively. Visually and stylistically, it’s sharp and well-directed, with a constant sense of tension building underneath even the lighter scenes. However, the story can feel divisive. While its bold twist and themes about honesty, relationships, and personal history are interesting, some viewers may find the narrative a bit uneven or provocative in a way that doesn’t fully resolve its ideas. It raises more questions than it answers, which works for some but may frustrate others. Overall, it’s a compelling, uneasy, and well-acted film that sticks with you, even if it’s not always comfortable or clean in its storytelling.

★★★★☆
